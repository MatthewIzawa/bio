# Strategy memo: Handling the 2022–2024 publication gap

*Captured from a brainstorming session on 2026-04-21.*

## Premise

Peer-reviewed publication output for 2022, 2023, and 2024 was zero, bracketed by productive years before (2010–2021 continuous) and recovery after (2025 onward). The gap reflects the COVID-19 research phase-lag compounded by health-related constraints. The strategic problem is presenting this honestly without drawing disproportionate attention to it in contexts where productivity will be assessed.

## Decisions locked in during brainstorm

- **Intent:** leaving Okayama. Priority order of affected documents: (D) grant biosketches → (A) academic faculty applications → (B) industry / national lab → (E) public website → (C) internal tenure/promotion, which is no longer of interest.
- **Openness policy:** venue-dependent (the "Option 4" position).
  - **Silence** on the public website.
  - **Impersonal phase-lag framing** as the default in grant biosketches, cover letters, and research statements: *"Research productivity during 2022–2024 was affected by the research phase-lag from the COVID-19 pandemic compounded by health-related constraints."*
  - **Only when an application explicitly solicits a career-disruption statement** is a marginally fuller version warranted. No diagnoses, no apology, one sentence.
- **General posture:** no over-explanation. Reviewers in planetary science recognise the 2022–24 phase-lag pattern because many share it.

## D — Grant biosketches (highest near-term priority)

The standard 5-year publication window in biosketches overlaps almost exactly with the gap. Silence magnifies the signal.

1. **Exploit the "most significant contributions" slot** that nearly every major funder provides (NASA ROSES, NSF, ERC, UKRI, JSPS, Horizon). That section is not year-restricted. Lead with five career-defining works drawn from the full record; candidates include the OSIRIS-REx Bennu work, Martian meteorite petrogenesis papers, the Tagish Lake carbonaceous chondrite series, titanite ichnofossils in basaltic glass, and the oldhamite/osbornite reduced-phase spectroscopy paper.
2. **Where a strict 5-year window is required**, populate it with 2025–2026 output. Current count: 3 peer-reviewed + 12 abstracts (several first-authored) is enough to read as an active record.
3. **Use the career-disruption statement section where the funder provides one** (NSF, ERC, UKRI, Horizon, increasingly JSPS). Single sentence, as above. No details.
4. **Lead with J-PEAKS (2023–2028, ¥5.5B consortium grant).** A major active funded consortium *awarded during the gap* is the single strongest counter-evidence that the community and funders treated the gap as temporary.
5. **Pipeline section where allowed** — submitted / in-review / in-preparation pubs as a separate labelled category. Publication timing lags work completion; this category captures work the review clock has not yet caught up to.

## A — Academic faculty applications

Search committees in the 2024–2026 hiring cycle are accustomed to COVID-era pub dips. A three-year trough is not an outlier; it is unremarkable enough that the *framing* rather than the *fact* determines reception.

1. **Cover letter carries the load in one sentence.** Use the default phase-lag framing, placed mid-letter in the trajectory paragraph, not the opening. Immediately pivot to 2025–2026 work and future plans at the target institution.
2. **Research statement leads with vision.** 2025–2026 output functions as evidence of capability; 2010–2021 arc demonstrates the trajectory being resumed.
3. **Publication list: use the `pubs:academic` generator export as-is.** Year ordering is expected in this document and reorganising by theme risks reading as avoidance.
4. **Surface 2022–2024 non-publication activity elsewhere in the CV** so a chronologically scanning reviewer sees activity in those years regardless of the pub column:
   - Active PhD supervision began/continued (Saji, Tian, Luo, Chijiwa Takamu).
   - NASA review panels (continuing since 2013, including 2022–2024).
   - OSIRIS-REx OLA Planetary Data System review panel.
   - J-PEAKS consortium grant awarded (2023).
   - Conference abstracts 2022–2024 (first-authored entries particularly valuable).
5. **Invited talks during 2022–2024, if any beyond the 2025 Chengdu lecture, belong prominently on the CV.** Invited talks during a pub gap are the best available counter-evidence of continued standing in the field.

## B — Industry / national lab applications

The gap is effectively invisible at this document granularity.

1. **Switch format entirely:** 1–2 page resume, not a CV. Skills- and impact-first.
2. **Mission experience headlines:** OSIRIS-REx, Dawn, MSL. This ranks above any individual publication for industry/lab evaluators.
3. **Representative publications only:** 5–10 highest-impact plus recent, not the full 82.
4. **No narrative about the gap** on the resume. If raised in interview, the same phase-lag sentence applies, said once, then moved on from.

## E — Public website (matthewizawa.github.io)

Lowest stakes.

1. **Optional:** one sentence on the bio page acknowledging 2022–2024 with the phase-lag framing. Saying nothing is equally defensible.
2. **Generator output needs no change.** Tabbed presentation already flattens the chronological trough visually relative to a year-by-year chart.
3. **Consider adding a "Current Projects" or "In Progress" section** to the site. Forward-looking content is the strongest antidote to a retrospective gap.

## One standard sentence to reuse

Draft default, adjustable per venue:

> Research productivity during 2022–2024 was affected by the research phase-lag from the COVID-19 pandemic compounded by health-related constraints; output has resumed at pre-2022 pace from 2025 onward.

Shorter variant (cover letters, biosketch character-limited fields):

> Output during 2022–2024 reflects the COVID-19 research phase-lag and health-related constraints; publications have resumed from 2025.
